COVID-19 Mortality Risk Model

Considerations

Our risk model uses the classifier method to narrow contributing factors with high feature importance for COVID-19 mortality predictions. The contributing factors are narrowed to age, gender, and the top 6 features of each category in pre-existing conditions and symptoms.

Logistic regression model is applied which shows an accuracy score of 0.88. Although the low-risk population f1-score is 92%, the high risk population f1-score is only 66%, showing higher precision and recall for low rather than high risk populations.

The confusion matrix shows that the logistic regression model is able to accurately identify 87.5% ((75% true low risk = predicted low risk) + (12.5% true high risk = predicted high risk)) of its test data into their respective risk categories which show strong predictive capabilities.
